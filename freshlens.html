Flagship Project · Weeks 3–8

FreshLens

Point your camera at a fridge, basket, or produce shelf—and every item lights up at once with a live, color-coded freshness score. Then AI tells you what to do about it: rescue what’s turning, or mark it down before it’s lost.

This summer the team builds one feature on top: Snap-to-Claim — a capped, evidence-backed, human-in-the-loop refund loop for perishables that arrive spoiled.

The leap

Expired Solutions already scans one item at a time and scores its freshness 0–100 across 135 produce types. FreshLens is the 10x: scan the whole scene at once. One tap over a fridge or a produce display, and every item is found, scored, and ranked—live.

That single capability serves both sides of the business. Pointed at a shopper’s fridge, it’s a magic “what do I eat first, and what can I cook tonight?” moment. Pointed at a store’s produce section, it’s an instant freshness audit that tells the manager exactly what to mark down—the heart of the markdown-automation pitch.

This summer’s build: Snap-to-Claim

On top of the FreshLens base, the team builds one focused feature for Weeks 3–8: Snap-to-Claim (Return Trust) — a capped, evidence-backed, human-in-the-loop refund loop for perishables that arrive spoiled.

A shopper let down by a discounted perishable snaps the item + receipt in-app. The Expired Solutions freshness model advises on condition. A capped policy decides — auto-approve a small, obvious case, or route it to a human. Every step is duplicate-checked, given a provenance receipt, and written to a tamper-evident audit log. The wedge isn’t the freshness score — it’s the closed, verifiable-evidence loop.

1

Advise

The model scores the photo 0–100 on the canonical scale (0 = fresh, 100 = spoiled/urgent). It’s an advisory input, never a guarantee — copy never says “our AI confirms this is spoiled”.

2

Decide (capped)

A deterministic policy auto-approves only the narrow obvious-defect band (and only after calibration); everything else goes to a human. A rolling $15 / 30-day cap limits exposure. The policy never auto-declines.

3

Prove

Every decision gets a provenance receipt that verifies and one hash-chained audit event. Cap + duplicate checks run before the model is ever called.

Four guardrails are non-negotiable: advisory language only · human-in-the-loop outside the narrow band · calibration before any auto-threshold · cap + duplicate checks before the model. Full detail in INITIATIVE-freshness-guarantee.md and the executable SPEC-claim-loop.md.

The claim flow

1 · SnapShopper photographs the item + receipt in-app.
→
2 · CheckCap + duplicate checks run first (before the model).
→
3 · AdviseModel scores condition (0 = fresh → 100 = urgent).
→
4 · DecideCapped policy: auto-approve or human-review.
→
5 · ProveReceipt verifies + audit event appended.

Mind the direction. The canonical FreshLens scale is 0 = perfectly fresh, 100 = spoiled / urgent (low = green/fresh, high = red/use-now). The raw model returns the opposite (high = fresh); the es_api adapter inverts it at the seam, so all claim code works on the canonical scale.

Architecture

A FastAPI service that puts a capped, audited policy around the freshness model. Beginners assemble proven parts rather than inventing them — exactly the skill the program teaches.

The build plan (Weeks 3–8)

One feature, built in vertical slices — each Friday ends with a live demo. Week 3 is the most important week: the calibration experiment gates everything after it.

Week 3 · Calibration + Walking Skeleton
  • Run 150–300 real phone photos through the model
  • Write the calibration gate (docs/CALIBRATION.md)
  • Stand up the minimal claim loop end-to-end (advisory)
  • Lock scope based on what calibration shows
Demo: calibration scorecard + one photo through the skeleton (scored → advisory decision → receipt verifies → audit event).
Week 4 · Policy Engine + Audit Trail
  • Deterministic adjudicate() tier decision
  • Rolling cap + duplicate detection
  • Hash-chained audit on every state change
Demo: obvious spoilage auto-approves under the cap; a duplicate → human-review; audit entry for each.
Week 5 · Evidence Quality
  • Receipt parsing + item-identity match
  • Fraud signals (item mismatch, recycled photo)
  • Capture-metadata validation
Demo: banana photo + “strawberry” receipt → human-review (item mismatch); a genuine match → auto-approve within cap.
Week 6 · Shopper UX + Business Review
  • Shopper claim wizard (in-app capture)
  • Store-manager review queue
  • Monthly-cap config panel
Demo: shopper four-screen claim end-to-end; manager review queue + cap config.
Week 7 · Provenance Hardening
  • Ed25519-signed receipts (verify without forging)
  • On-chain anchor of the receipt digest
  • Optional track: blockchain provenance receipts
Demo: signed receipt verifies green → tamper one character → verifies red; audit shows the receipt ID.
Week 8 · Integration, Freeze & Demo Day
  • Wire the loop into the host app UI
  • Freeze, record the demo
  • Present on Demo Day
Demo: shopper claim → capped decision → receipt verifies; business review → approve → download the audit log.

Full week-by-week missions live in the repo’s docs/ (WEEK-03.md … WEEK-08.md).

This week — Week 3: what to do

Two threads: (a) the calibration experiment that decides whether the model is trustworthy enough to ever auto-approve, and (b) the walking skeleton that proves the claim loop runs end-to-end. Nine starter tasks are filed as GitHub issues — nobody is preassigned; pick the one that fits you.

B

Walking skeleton

Policy stub, human-review-only (#30); POST /api/claims + evaluate (#35); the hash-chain audit (#31); skeleton tests (#36). These start on day one.

C

Design

Claim-flow wireframe v0 (#37) — how a shopper moves through snap → decision → receipt.

Grab one with gh issue develop <N> --checkout (that’s how you claim it, so two people don’t collide). Detail + acceptance criteria: WEEK-03.md and WEEK-01-ISSUES.md. Get started

The starter repo is a complete, runnable app — clone it and it works on day one, offline, with no API key (placeholder freshness scorer). The Getting Started guide takes you from clone to running in your browser in ~15 minutes; then Week 3 (docs/WEEK-03.md) is your first mission. To use the real Expired Solutions model you point the app at the read-only proxy with your own intern key (handed out privately) — see docs/ES-MODEL-ACCESS.md. No prior AI-tools experience needed; the repo includes a guide for using Claude Code well (and keeping it cheap on a $20 plan).
