Provenance Receipts

Turn a produce scan into a signed, tamper-evident receipt and anchor its fingerprint on a public blockchain—so a return or refund can be verified later, without trusting anyone’s word.

SHA-256 Digital signatures Ethereum Sepolia FastAPI FSMA 204

The pain point

Expired Solutions already offers a no-questions-asked 24-hour refund on discounted perishables—a real trust advantage, and a real fraud surface.

With produce there is nothing tying a specific item to a specific purchase. A barcode says what type it is—not which store sold this unit, when, or in what condition. So a soggy item returned days later is a he-said-she-said; a receipt can be reused; and an item bought at one store can be “returned” at another. Across US retail, roughly 15% of 2024 returns—about $103B—were fraudulent. Perishables can’t be resold, so every disputed return is a write-off. We want the trust without the friction.

How it works

Four steps. The first two give you a tamper-evident receipt; the third makes it independently checkable by anyone; the fourth is the return desk.

1

Hash

SHA-256 of the photo plus the scan facts—store, lot code, UTC time, freshness score, items—into one canonical fingerprint.

2

Sign

Sign the fingerprint so any later edit is detectable and the issuer is provable. HMAC today; an Ed25519 upgrade lets outside parties verify too.

3

Anchor

Write the 32-byte fingerprint to a smart contract on the Ethereum Sepolia testnet—a public, timestamped record nobody can quietly rewrite.

4

Verify

At return time, re-hash the presented receipt and check the signature (issuer) and the anchor (existence + time). Valid → refund with confidence.

The refund journey

1 · ScanShopper scans the produce at pickup.
→
2 · SignFreshLens signs the scan into a receipt.
→
3 · AnchorIts fingerprint is anchored on-chain.
→
4 · PresentDays later, the shopper opens a return.
→
5 · VerifyRe-hash & check → valid, or tampered.

A fraud attempt fails cleanly: edit the freshness score or swap the store on a saved receipt and the fingerprint no longer matches, so verification returns digest_mismatch and the refund is declined. A fabricated receipt has no valid signature or anchor.

What it proves — and what it doesn’t

It proves: this exact receipt existed at (or before) a point in time, and was issued by a holder of the signing key. Any tampering breaks verification.

It does not prove that the photo is of the real item, or that the freshness score is correct—garbage in, signed garbage out. It only proves the receipt’s existence, time, and issuer. With the starter’s placeholder detector every item is a generic “produce”; real per-item provenance arrives once the detector and freshness model are wired (Weeks 4–5).

On-chain is permanent: an anchored record cannot be deleted. That’s exactly why we anchor only a 32-byte fingerprint—never the receipt contents or any personal data.

Architecture

A small FastAPI feature that signs a scan and anchors its fingerprint. It runs offline out of the box (a local hash-chain stands in for the chain); interns wire the real testnet anchor in Week 7.

A signed hash already delivers the trust for a single store verifying its own receipts; the public anchor earns its place when an outside party (another retailer, an insurer, a court) must verify without trusting that store’s server. Large enterprise food-traceability blockchains proved supply-side provenance works; the lesson for a startup is a lightweight anchor, not a consortium chain.

Beyond refunds

The same signed, timestamped, lot-linked receipt unlocks more than returns.

Recall management

Find every receipt carrying a recalled lot_code — a targeted recall instead of a blanket category pull.

Chain of custody

Each supplier→store handoff issues a signed condition receipt; “arrived already bad” disputes arbitrate from evidence.

Shrink & markdown audit

A signed markdown event (item, photo, manager, time) deters markdown fraud and feeds loss analysis.

Insurance claims

Timestamped, photo-backed condition records make spoilage claims faster and harder to abuse.

Loyalty & transparency

“This lot received here on the 12th, in good condition” — a freshness story a shopper can scan.

FSMA 204 alignment

The lot_code is the same Traceability Lot Code the FDA food-traceability rule mandates upstream (compliance ~2028).

This is an optional Week-7 FreshLens extension: it ships runnable today with a local mock anchor (and a green test suite), and you make the blockchain real by anchoring on Sepolia. It’s demo-ware to verify the concept—production hardening comes later.
